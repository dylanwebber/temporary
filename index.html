How Financially Stable are you?

Things can always turn South real quick. If an economic downturn hit tomorrow, where would you be standing? The answer is not well. No one would be. But you can at least be better prepared than nothing! It's not an easy task, but it's necessary if you want to be safe and stable come the unfortunate time you lose your job or the economy takes a turn for the worst. Read here and figure out where you stand and how you could improve!

The first thing you should do each and every time you receive your paycheck is put a portion of it into your savings. That should be habitual. In all honesty, you shouldn't even have to think about it. Through most banks, you can set this up automatically. If something happens to you, you'll always want a savings fund to lean on.

The future is key when it comes to trying to make yourself financially stable. If your employer offers a 401K, put as much in as your employer is willing to match. If you can't afford to max it, at least put a portion of your paycheck in there. If your employer doesn't offer a 401K, open up a Roth IRA. Most banks and brokerages offer some variation of one, so open one up today to start slowly building future funds!

Create a plan to get rid of debt. Debt follows you wherever you go and will never leave you alone. In a lot of cases, it can get worse. Credit card debt, student loans, whatever it is, you need to be building a plan to get rid of it. It's unreasonable to think you can cancel all your debt in one paycheck, so you have to build a plan to help offset it. Like you do with your savings deposits, you should put a monthly portion of your paycheck into paying off your debt. Depending on the kind, you'll want to either expedite the process or stretch it out over a longer time period.

Lastly, you should make it a big emphasis to pay your bills and rent off immediately each month. This is a basic part of your expenditure that must be paid each month. If you wait to pay it off, several things could happen. One, your mind is tricked into thinking you have more money available to you than you actually do. When you pay off your bills immediately, it becomes as if you never even had that money. Secondly, you can miss your bill payments and get dinged on your credit score. If you start taking hits to your credit score, the rest of your financial future becomes way more difficult.
